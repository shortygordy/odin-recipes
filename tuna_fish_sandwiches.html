Tuna Fish Sandwiches

What are Tuna Fish Sandwiches?

Well, it sounds exactly like what it's called. Its a sandwich, with a filler of a tuna fish salad. This is a recipe loosely based on my mother's during my childhood. Those were the best lunchtime meal, especially with a loaf of freshly baked French bread from the local Winco Food's bakery. I've added a few ingredients to spice things up, so let's get started in prepping this quick and easy, but delicious afternoon meal!

Ingredients

  • 4 cans of Tune (in oil)
  • 1/4 cup of mayonnaise
  • Minced dill pickles
  • Garlic
  • Red onion
  • Lemon juice
  • Salt & Pepper

Instructions

  1. Add the drained tuna to a bowl. Stir in the rest of your ingredients : Mayo, celery, onion, pickle relish, garlic, and lemon juice. Mix well and season to taste with salt and pepper.
  2. Heat a skillet over medium-high heat. Meanwhile, butter one each side of two slices of bread. I like a white or wheat bread with lots of toast-able surface area.
  3. Lay one piece of bread in the skillet and top with a thick layer of your tuna fish salad. Top with a piece of cheese (preferably American) and a second piece of buttered bread (butter-side up).
  4. When the bottom bread is golden brown, carefully flip the entire tuna melt. Continue cooking until the second side is browned. Remove and devour!
